Field Guide to Rare and Common Clouds

A selection of clouds of interest to the casual cloud spotter

Cumulus

These low level clouds develop on days with clear skies. They will appear in the morning, will grow during the day and dissolve by evening.

Cumulonimbus

These low level clouds can produce rain, thunder, hail and tornadoes.

Alto Cumulous

These are the most common types of clouds, they are mid level clouds that will appear with other cloud types and can be layered on top of each other.

Volutus

These clouds are related to stratocumulous and sometimes altocumulous clouds. They appear to roll on a horizontal axis and do not connect to other clouds.

Castelanus

An altocumulous cloud that has tower like forms that rise from a common base.

Lenticularis

A type of alto cumulous cloud that is formed by the air currents around mountains and tall buildings.
